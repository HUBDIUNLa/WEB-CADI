Product and Service Design

What is Product or Service Design?

Product and service design are two essential approaches within industrial design. Both focus on improving people’s experiences, either through tangible objects or more complex interaction systems.

Product Design focuses on creating physical objects that are functional, aesthetic, and manufacturable. It involves decisions about materials, manufacturing processes, ergonomics, sustainability, and technical feasibility. A good product design addresses real needs, optimizes resources, and provides value to both users and the market.

Service Design expands the scope of industrial design toward broader and more intangible experiences. It focuses on planning, structuring, and improving services by considering all stakeholders, touchpoints, and user journeys. This approach enables people-centered solutions that are efficient, accessible, and memorable.

Both types of design are complementary and mutually reinforcing. Now more than ever, products and services must be conceived in an integrated way to generate innovative, sustainable, and high-impact proposals.
