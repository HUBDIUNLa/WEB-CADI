What is a Design Diagnosis?

The Design Diagnosis is a process aimed at evaluating and analyzing the current state of a project or venture, with the goal of identifying areas for improvement and opportunities for innovation. Through a comprehensive approach, it seeks to optimize processes, products, and services to ensure their viability and competitiveness in the market.

The Design Diagnosis is a tool designed to evaluate products or services from an integral industrial design perspective. Through a series of guiding questions and image analysis, it helps identify improvement opportunities related to functionality, aesthetics, communication, and production.

This process allows the detection of strengths, weaknesses, and concrete action proposals to enhance the development of products, brands, or processes. It's not just about correcting errors but about supporting strategic decision-making to achieve more efficient, sustainable solutions aligned with user needs.

The diagnosis is intended for students, entrepreneurs, cooperatives, and professionals looking to evaluate or redirect their projects with the support of a team specialized in design, innovation, and product development.
